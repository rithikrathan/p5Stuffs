Get the Outline:

        Open your image.

        Go to Filters > Edge-Detect > Edge... (use "Sobel" or similar).

        Colors > Threshold. Adjust until you have a stark black-and-white outline.

        Image > Mode > Indexed (Select "Use black and white (1-bit) palette"). This ensures pixels are purely true white (255) or true black (0).

    Connect the Islands (Crucial):

        Identify "floating" parts (like eyes, nose holes, mouth).

        Use the Pencil Tool (1 pixel size).

        Draw a "bridge" line connecting the main head outline to the eye, trace the eye, and then trace back (or just leave the bridge).

        Note: The DFT will draw this bridge line. If you want it invisible, it requires complex math later. For now, just accept the bridge lines (like a neon sign that connects letters).

    Thin the Line:

        If your line is thick (2-3 pixels), the "walker" algorithm will get confused.

        Search for a "Skeletonize" plugin for GIMP, or manually erase pixels until the line is exactly 1 pixel wide everywhere.

Step 2: Extract Ordered Coordinates (The "Walker")

GIMP cannot export an "ordered" array by default; it only sees a grid of pixels. You need a script that "walks" the path to generate the x and y arrays in the correct drawing order.
